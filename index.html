📉 How Capitalism Fuels Inequality

Corporations seek the lowest costs and highest profits—often at the expense of human dignity. The global South becomes a staging ground for extractive labor: minimum wage, no protection, and maximum output.

"Today, over 160 million children are involved in labor globally, many in supply chains of Western brands." – ILO, 2023

🔗 Global Supply Chains and Realities

Outsourcing shields brands from accountability. Multiple tiers of suppliers blur the lines of legal responsibility, creating a gray zone where violations occur unchecked. Most large companies operate with over 5,000 suppliers globally.

"Only 27% of Fortune 500 companies monitor Tier 2 suppliers for labor violations." – Ethical Trade Report

📍 Case Studies of Corporate Negligence

  • Rana Plaza, Bangladesh: 1,134 garment workers killed due to structural collapse while producing for major Western brands.
  • Foxconn, China: Series of suicides among iPhone assembly workers sparked international outrage over intense factory conditions.
  • Hershey, Ivory Coast: Linked to cocoa plantations employing child labor in hazardous conditions with no formal oversight.
  • Nike, Indonesia: Protests exposed poor wages and forced overtime; sparked major anti-sweatshop movements in the 90s.

💡 Why This Matters

  • 🌐 Billions depend on low-wage factory work to survive
  • 🛍️ Western lifestyles are built on cheap labor from the Global South
  • 💥 Crises like COVID-19 reveal how fragile and unfair the system is
  • 📣 Accountability is a choice, not an accident

🚀 Toward a Fairer Future

  • ✅ Enforceable international labor standards
  • 🔍 Transparent sourcing laws and worker visibility
  • 🧰 Empowered global labor unions
  • 🌱 Sustainable production over disposable consumerism
  • 🧠 Conscious consumer movements that pressure brands into ethical reforms
