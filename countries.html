📍 Countries Exploited by Globalization

Deep dives into labor realities across the Global South

⬅ Back to Overview

🇵🇭 Philippines

Known for call centers and electronics assembly, Filipino workers face precarious contracts, wage suppression, and anti-union repression. Government-endorsed labor export further normalizes overseas worker exploitation.

🇨🇳 China

China’s rapid industrial rise was fueled by cheap labor. From Foxconn suicides to factory fires, intense production demands and weak rights protections have defined much of the export economy’s human cost.

🇮🇩 Indonesia

Global brands rely on Indonesia’s low wages for garment and shoe production. Weak labor inspections and union crackdowns have led to systemic rights violations.

🇻🇳 Vietnam

Now one of the largest textile exporters, Vietnam’s one-party state suppresses independent unionization. Workers operate under strict discipline and surveillance with minimal bargaining power.

🇲🇾 Malaysia

Migrant workers in Malaysia’s electronics and palm oil industries report withheld wages, confiscated passports, and debt bondage—especially among Nepali, Bangladeshi, and Burmese workers.

🇹🇭 Thailand

Thailand’s seafood and agriculture industries employ trafficked migrants under exploitative contracts. Cases of slavery and wage theft have been widely documented.

🇲🇲 Myanmar

Post-coup conditions worsened labor rights. Garment factories contracted by Western brands exploit political instability to suppress wages and silence organizing.

🇨🇬 DR Congo

Vital to global tech through cobalt mining, Congo’s child miners work in life-threatening conditions for global battery supply chains, often without basic safety or compensation.

🇿🇲 Zambia

Copper and mineral wealth in Zambia benefit foreign mining firms. Workers face toxic exposure and low pay while Chinese and Western investors reap profits.

🇿🇼 Zimbabwe

Years of economic turmoil have left Zimbabwean workers desperate. Foreign tobacco and diamond companies use the chaos to source cheap labor with minimal oversight.

🇭🇹 Haiti

Garment workers in Haiti earn some of the lowest wages in the Western Hemisphere. Brands like Gildan and Hanes exploit weak governance and dire poverty.

🇧🇷 Brazil

In Brazil’s Amazon and rural zones, agricultural workers face slave-like conditions. Informal labor in meatpacking and sugar cane cutting is widespread.

🇲🇽 Mexico

NAFTA led to a boom in maquiladoras near the U.S. border. These low-wage factories suppress unionization and often operate in hazardous conditions.

🇸🇳 Senegal

Senegalese fishers are increasingly sidelined by European fleets under trade deals. Local fishworkers face unemployment and migration while foreign firms overfish their waters.

🇲🇦 Morocco

Fruit and vegetable exports rely on migrant labor under poor housing and wage theft conditions. Women laborers are especially vulnerable in these sectors.

🇳🇵 Nepal

Nepal is a major source of migrant labor for Gulf countries and Malaysia. Many Nepalis endure harsh treatment, withheld pay, and fatal working conditions in construction and domestic work.

🇺🇬 Uganda

Agro-export sectors like coffee and tea often exploit child labor and pay below subsistence levels. Regulatory enforcement is weak, especially in rural areas.

🇹🇿 Tanzania

In the mining sector, Tanzanian laborers face unsafe working environments while gold and gemstones are extracted for global luxury markets.

🇱🇰 Sri Lanka

Garment workers face intensified pressure amid economic crisis and inflation. Labor laws exist but enforcement is under-resourced and often ignored.

🇧🇴 Bolivia

In Bolivian agriculture and mining, foreign companies benefit from weak safety standards and labor informality. Coca leaf and lithium exploitation remain hotbeds of concern.
